Answering the following questions

  1. PEG is good for real-life photos with many colors and details, like pictures of people, places, or nature.
  2. GIF is good for simple images with few colors, like logos, icons, drawings, and small animations.
  3. PNG supports transparent backgrounds and keeps the image quality clear without losing details.
  4. Open the photo in photo editing software, change it to the size you need, and save it before using it. You can use a website like Pixlr to resize the image. In Windows, there is a built-in option to resize images. On a Mac, you can use the Preview app. These options help you resize the image easily.
